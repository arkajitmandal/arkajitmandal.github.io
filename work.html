Research

We developed an on-the-fly quantum dynamics approach that resolves a long-standing incompatibility challenge, for combining any diabatic quantum dynamics approach with most of the electronic structure methods that outputs in the adiabatic representation, for simulating photoinduced reactions in molecular systems. The QD approach has enabled us to simulate and gain mechanistic insights into complex reactions like the proton coupled electron transfer reaction. Our method, the Quasi-Diabatic (QD) propagation scheme, extends the applicability of both the electronic structure methods as well as the existing diabatic quantum dynamics approaches and facilitates the merger between the respective communities.



In polaritonic photochemistry the quantum nature of photons is harnessed to enable new photochemical reactivities. We developed new theoretical tools and analytical theories to investigate new chemical reactivities enabled by coupling quantized photons to molecular systems. With these tools we provide time-dependent mechanistic insights and demonstrate that the photon can provide new control knobs (like the photon frequency) to precisely control chemical reactions while the photons themselves can be used as a catalysts to trigger multiple photochemical reactions.
